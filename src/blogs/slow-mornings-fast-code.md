Title: Slow Mornings, Fast Code
Date: 2026-04-12

I used to think productivity meant starting the day at a sprint. Coffee, inbox, standup, code — all before my brain had finished booting. It took a repetitive strain injury and a very patient physiotherapist to teach me otherwise.

## The Experiment

For thirty days I tried the opposite: no screen for the first hour. Just tea, a paper notebook, and whatever book was on the table.

The results were annoyingly predictable:

- I wrote **less** code but shipped **more** features
- Bugs I'd been circling for days dissolved on the walk to my desk
- My commit messages became legible to other humans

## Why It Works (Probably)

```
attention(morning) = focus_budget - notifications_consumed
```

That's not real math, but it might as well be. Every ping I answer before thinking costs me the deep-work window where the hard problems actually get solved.

## The Catch

Slow mornings require a fast evening — I prep everything the night before, or the morning becomes a scramble and the whole experiment collapses. Discipline in one place buys freedom in another. It always does.
