Title: The Books That Changed Me
Date: 2026-07-02

There are books you read, and there are books that read you. This is a small list of the second kind — the ones that left fingerprints on how I think.

## The Quiet Ones

Some books don't announce themselves. You pick them up on a rainy afternoon, expecting nothing, and three hours later you're a slightly different person.

> A reader lives a thousand lives before he dies. The man who never reads lives only one.

That quote gets thrown around a lot, but it earned its clichédom honestly.

## What Stays

What stays with me is rarely the plot. It's a sentence here, an image there:

- The smell of a library described so well I could breathe it
- A character's small act of kindness in an otherwise cruel chapter
- An ending that refused to resolve, and was braver for it

## Reading as Practice

I've started treating reading less like consumption and more like practice — the way a musician practices scales. Some days it's effortless. Some days every page is a climb. Both days count.

If you keep a list of books that changed you, I'd love to compare notes. My Goodreads is in the footer.
