Title: Letters to My Past Self
Date: 2026-03-08

If I could mail one letter back through time, the postage would be worth any price. Since I can't, I'll leave it here, where past versions of other people might find it.

## Dear Eighteen-Year-Old Me

You don't have to pick one thing forever. The people telling you to "specialize early" are describing their own fears, not your future. The weird detours — the semester of philosophy, the failed band, the summer spent reading manga instead of textbooks — those become the interesting parts of the story.

## Dear Twenty-Two-Year-Old Me

The job you didn't get was a favor. You can't see it yet, and you won't for three years. Keep the friends who visited you when you were broke. They are the actual wealth.

## Dear Last-Year Me

Back up the journal. Yes, the whole thing. Tonight.

## The Trick of It

Writing these letters, I noticed something: every piece of advice is really just forgiveness with a timestamp. You couldn't have known. You did the best you could with the map you had.

Which means the letter future me is writing right now probably says the same thing about today. That's oddly comforting. Carry on.
