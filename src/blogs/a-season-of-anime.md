Title: A Season of Anime, Reviewed Honestly
Date: 2026-06-18

Every season I tell myself I'll only follow two shows. Every season I end up with seven. Here's an honest accounting of the current one.

## The Keeper

One show this season justified the entire medium for me. No spoilers, but there's an episode that spends twenty minutes on a single conversation — no action, no music for long stretches — and it's the most gripping thing I've watched all year.

## The Comfort Watch

Not everything needs to be a masterpiece. There's a slice-of-life show airing right now that does absolutely nothing new, and I look forward to it more than anything else in my week. Comfort is a feature, not a bug.

## The One I Dropped

I gave it four episodes. The premise was brilliant, the execution was a PowerPoint presentation. Life is short and the backlog is long.

## Scoring Philosophy

My AniList scores confuse people, so here's the rubric:

1. Did it make me feel something?
2. Did it respect my time?
3. Would I rewatch it?

Three yeses is a 9 or 10. Two is a 7. One is a 5. Zero means I probably didn't finish it — and that's okay.
