Title: On Keeping a Jeevankatha
Date: 2026-05-30

*Jeevankatha* — a life story. It sounds grand, but mine is mostly small entries about ordinary days. That's the point.

## Why Write It Down

Memory is a terrible archivist. It keeps the embarrassments in high definition and lets the good ordinary days blur together. Writing is my way of filing a protest.

The entries that mean the most to me now are the boring ones:

- What I ate on a random Tuesday two years ago
- A conversation with a friend I've since lost touch with
- The exact feeling of finishing a project at 2 AM

## The Rules I Keep

I have exactly three rules for this journal:

1. **No performance.** If I'm writing for an audience, it goes on the blog. The journal is for me.
2. **No skipping guilt.** Missed a month? Fine. Start again. The streak is not the point.
3. **Date everything.** Future me always wants to know *when*.

## Public vs Private

This site is the public edge of that practice — the entries I don't mind sharing. Think of it as the director's commentary on a film you'll never see. Somehow, that's enough.
